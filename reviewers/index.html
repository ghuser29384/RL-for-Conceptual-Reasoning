Step 2 of 3

Rubric and consent

Evaluate the critique—not whether you agree with the position. Scores run from 0 to 1.

Centrality

Does the critique address an important part of the position?

Strength

If correct, how much would the critique weaken the position?

Correctness

How accurate are the critique’s claims, weighted by importance?

Clarity

Is the critique easy to understand and sufficiently precise?

Dead weight

How much of the critique is irrelevant, redundant, or unhelpful?

Single issue

Does the critique stay focused on one issue?

Overall

Considering the other dimensions, how good is the critique?

Blinding rule. You will not be told whether a critique was written by a person or a model. Do not try to infer authorship; judge only the argument shown.

Step 3 of 3

Public calibration item

This synthetic item is used only for onboarding and qualification. It is not part of a hidden benchmark.

Position

Any public policy that reduces preventable deaths without imposing comparable harms ought to be adopted. Requiring smoke alarms in newly built homes reduces preventable deaths without imposing comparable harms. Therefore, smoke alarms ought to be required in newly built homes.

Critique

The first premise merely states a factual relationship about reducing deaths. The conclusion says what ought to be done. Because the argument never supplies a moral bridge from facts to obligations, it commits the is–ought fallacy.

Frequently asked questions

Who counts as an expert?

Relevant backgrounds include philosophy, AI safety, ethics, decision theory, political theory, law, economics, cognitive science, and closely related fields. Doctoral students and researchers with comparable professional experience are eligible.

Will the calibration item enter the research benchmark?

No. It is a synthetic public onboarding item. Hidden evaluation material is kept separate from recruitment and training.

How is payment determined?

The $25 calibration payment is available after identity, eligibility, duplicate, and good-faith completion checks. Submissions generated by a bot, copied from another person, or completed with external AI assistance are not eligible.

What data may be published?

Calibration responses and reviewer metadata may be analyzed in deidentified form. Public acknowledgment by name is optional and requires an explicit opt-in.
