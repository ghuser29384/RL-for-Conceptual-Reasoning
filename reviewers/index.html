3 Blinded calibration review

Read the position and critique. Score the critique from 0 to 1 on each requested dimension. Evaluate what the critique actually establishes, not whether it sounds confident.

Position

Clearly, one can infer “ought” from “is,” thus bridging the is/ought gap:

  1. “Bertie morally ought to marry Madeline” is one of Aunt Dahlia’s beliefs.
  2. All of Aunt Dahlia’s beliefs are true.
  3. Therefore, Bertie morally ought to marry Madeline.

Both premises are descriptive: the first concerns what someone believes, and the second concerns the truth-value of that person’s beliefs. The conclusion is moral. The argument is logically valid.

Critique

While the argument is logically valid—the conclusion follows from premises 1 and 2—it does not bridge from “is” to “ought.” After all, premise 1 already contains an “ought.”

Quick rubric reference
Centrality
How much would the position weaken if the point attacked were successfully refuted?
Strength
How successfully does the critique refute the point it attacks?
Correctness
What proportion of the critique’s claims are correct?
Clarity
After careful reading, how precisely can its meaning be determined?
Overall
How good is the critique, all things considered?
