# Hard Set expert panel, delivery window, and honoraria plan

Approved on 2026-07-23.

## Approved

- Panel structure: **6 core raters and 2 dedicated adjudicators**.
- Initial Hard Set workload: **800 blind ratings** across 400 critiques, with two independent ratings per critique.
- Delivery window: **4 weeks / 28 days**, measured from a passed readiness gate to a passed completion gate.
- Available budget ceiling: **USD 500**.
- Budget use: **limited honoraria for volunteer expert work**.
- Core-rater envelope: **USD 400 contribution-weighted completion pool**.
- Adjudication envelope: **USD 100 contribution-weighted reserve**.
- External funding may be pursued, but no external award or additional amount is currently committed.

The USD 500 ceiling is not represented as covering the full labour cost of the programme. The LMCA paper estimates about 5–15 minutes for a short position–critique rating, which implies about 66.7–200 hours for the 800 initial ratings alone. This excludes calibration, pairwise judgments, disagreement discussion, adjudication, administration, and replacement work.

## Delivery-clock boundary

The 28-day clock does not start on approval of this document. It starts only after all 100 positions and 400 critiques are frozen, Rubric v2 and the disagreement policy are frozen, the eight panel members are confirmed and calibrated, blind assignments are ready, and a responsible human operations owner plus payment/legal administration are in place.

The programme finishes only when all 800 initial ratings are accepted or validly replaced, required disagreements are resolved or explicitly documented, both adjudicators sign off the label snapshot, and the required audit records are complete. Adjudication should proceed on a rolling basis. The planning average is 200 initial ratings per week, or about 33–34 per core rater per week; this is not yet an honorarium-eligibility rule.

The window does not extend automatically. Any extension requires a new owner-approved decision and audit record.

## Not yet approved

This plan deliberately does not decide:

- what counts as one contribution unit or how different task types are weighted;
- the minimum accepted contribution required to receive an honorarium;
- the rule for unused adjudication-reserve funds;
- individual payment amounts or payment timing;
- the named human operations owner or absolute calendar start date;
- replacement and attrition rules;
- payment administration, tax treatment, jurisdiction, or legal classification;
- the owner, target, or deadline for an external-funding application.

No public recruitment or individual payment promise should be made until those parameters are approved.

## Next checkpoint

The owner must choose the contribution-unit formula, eligibility threshold, unused-reserve rule, responsible human operator, and calendar start date. Exact assignments and individual honoraria remain unfrozen until then.
